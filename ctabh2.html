Intro Scene

Welcome to Katong’s old-school bakery Chin Mee Chin Confectionery, famous for its charcoal-toasted kaya buns and pastries like Swiss rolls and cream puffs, which are best enjoyed with a cup of kopi.

You are Kelly, part of the Tan family running the business and are now the next generation, who has inherited your family legacy of running the quaint shop.

You're faced with a series of dilemmas when deciding what to do with the shop.....

There are suggestions from the partnership company to renovate the façade of the building, to have a more modern flair so as to attract millennials.

However, your grandparents have voiced out their concerns, stating that it is important to have the façade remain as it is, for that is the most authentic.

Uncle Tan: Developing may cause you to lose touch with your roots!!

Uncle Tan: Who are we without heritage, our roots define us. The facade is the heart of the shop.

Mr Swee: Having a modern flair, while keeping the authentic interior might show we are able to adapt with the times...

Mr Swee: A blend of the old and the new

Do you want to completely revamp the exterior to white, minimalist and modern or keep it all the same?

CONFIRM

Yes, you are one of culture.

Dang, a golden opportunity missed!

No one will recognise the shop anymore...

As they say, change is the only constant.

The interior of the current shop is kept true to your great grandparent's time. Old school tilings that have withered the test of time, with vintage furniture, that strikes familiarity.

When you take over the shop, you contemplate if the interiors should be more like the instagram cafes your generation visits.

You're met with a choice of an interior modern overhaul vs keeping a vintage aesthetic.

Uncle Tan: Social media trends are temporal, culture is forever.

Uncle Tan: Preserving the our vintage interiors, may be more valuable than fleeting trends.

Mr Swee: Interiors make up a big part of why people haunt these eateries, you want yours to be splashed over instagram as a "photo worthy" spot, don't you?

What is your decision?

CONFIRM

I'm glad you appreciate what those before us have done.

I hope you know what you're doing...

This dosen't feel like what we had...

We must stay on our toes or we'll fall behind, relevancy is key

Pinterest and Instagram are filled with many colorful pictures of confectioneries, and you wonder if you ought to do the same.

But it is the traditional layout of the food was passed on throughout the generations, and it is a key feature makes the shop so unique, so you wonder what to do.

Uncle Tan: Hipster food are seasonal, traditional food is culture.

Uncle Tan: Preserving our Hainanese culture through our food, may be more valuable than temporal fad. We got so many old customers! We cannot let them down!

Mr Swee: Hipster food is the way to go! How can you attract the new generation with old-fashioned food? No one eats them anymore!

Do you want to change your pastries completely to a new trendy food item? Or keep it the way it is?

CONFIRM

Good choice, now it wont be lost to just a memory.

If only you know what you're missing out on!

Now it's just like those other shops across the streets...

Smart move, this is how we survive the test of time.

A major developer has plans to turn the block of shophouses into a luxury hotel. He has approached other shophouse owners and needs a collective decision.

An en bloc sale, you're told by the developer. Your family will make a major profit by selling the plot due to it's location in Katong. The value, it will economically generate is also far greater than your current shop so he is willing to pay a high price and give u a cut in the hotel. You're faced with a tough choice as this has been in your line for generations...

Uncle Tan: Say NO!

Uncle Tan: Your children need to inherit the store and it will go on for future generations, don't waste the hard work done by your ancestors... for a commercial hotel.

Uncle Tan: The conservation value the building will offer later is far greater than the one now.

Mr Swee: Sell! Sell! Sell!

Mr Swee: Maybe, instead of giving away the whole property, chin mee chin can become part of the hotel, preserving the cafe and our interiors but revamped into a new premise.

Mr Swee: This gives us the revenue to keep running for generations to come.

Sell or keep the legacy alive?

CONFIRM

You make me proud, heritage is authenticity.

You are giving away your family's stability for some nostalgia...

Not many families can say that they own a shophouse, and you would just exchange it for money!

Heritage will still be a part of us, and so will our changing future.

Preserve the legacy...

You take over the shop, and leave it exactly as it is right now. Everything is conserved with no revamp. This is great for your family as everyone feels they have a piece of the old singapore in a new hip area!

However, the cost that comes with this decision of conservation is one you will have to bear. Patrons you attract are the same older crowd and revenue is stagnant. You're only able to break even every month.

This shows us that although conservation is socially profitable, for the individual, it may not be the best economic decision, for it is non adaptive to our changing times.

You've reached a middle!

The outcome of your decision making has led you to a slight impasse. This shows us that in issues of conservation, it is possible to have more radical stances in micro issues. (Eg your stance on the interior/exterior)

However, when the choices are then compiled and put into a bigger picture, the situation often leads to us making a compromise.

These compromises and middle ground show us that conservation issues are not as simple as a yes or no, many factors weigh in.

Complete new life!!!

Everything is brand new and spanking clean, young customers stream in with the online hype. Business is booming and you are able to even open more chains.

However, when family visits, they are not even able to recognise the old establishment, it's become completely foreign and blends in with the new swarm of cafes.

The economic decision is one that is profitable for your business model, however the social fabric that is tied to the place is lost. The older crowd stops coming as it has lost the appeal, the cafe starts anew to build a modern brand.
